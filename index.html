Scope & Call for Papers

Today’s data-centric distributed systems are evolving into large‑scale, highly heterogeneous edge swarms that must operate autonomously, preserve data sovereignty, and be demonstrably trustworthy. SwarmAware aims to bring together researchers and practitioners exploring new programming paradigms, middleware, verification techniques, and real‑world deployments that make decentralized, adaptive edge intelligence safe and reliable.

Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Trust and explainability in swarm intelligence
  • Formal methods for decentralized systems
  • Edge/IoT orchestration and federation
  • Self‑adaptive and self‑healing architectures
  • Swarm learning, FL, and collective perception
  • Blockchain and DLT for trustworthy autonomy
  • Testbeds, benchmarks, and real‑world case studies

Submission Guidelines

We welcome regular research papers (up to 8 pages) and position/vision papers (up to 4 pages), both following the standard ACM sigconf format. Submissions must be anonymous and will undergo double‑blind review by at least three experts.

  • PDFs via EasyChair
  • Accepted papers published in the ACM DL
  • At least one author must register and present
